Our Story

PHANTOM VI was established by Vincent Makafu in 2015 in the Free State. Starting from humble beginnings, Vincent faced many challenges but his vision and determination drove him forward.

From ashes to glory, Vincent grew PHANTOM VI steadily. Today, he operates from Colesberg, bringing custom wine, gin, and vodka bottles to discerning customers across the region. His passion for quality and innovation makes him the best in the game.

PHANTOM VI is more than just a brand , it’s a journey of resilience, creativity, and success. We’re proud to share our story with you and invite you to be part of our growing family.

Licensing & Legal Reality in South Africa

Navigating South Africa's liquor licensing landscape can be challenging. It's crucial not to rush into the licensing process. Phantom VI offers expert advice to help you understand the regulations and requirements.

In the interim, you can sell your product under Phantom VI's existing license. This allows you to showcase your brand at events, venues, or as corporate gifts, without the immediate burden of obtaining your own license.

Please Note: This is for promotional and demonstration purposes only and must adhere to South African liquor laws.

When you're ready to obtain your own liquor license, Phantom VI will guide you through the process.

  • Main Licensing Bodies: The primary licensing bodies are the provincial liquor boards.
  • Costs: Licensing costs vary by province and the type of license required. Expect application fees, inspection fees, and annual license renewal fees.
  • Process: The licensing process typically involves submitting an application, undergoing inspections, and attending hearings.
  • Investment & Timeline:
    • Estimated Total Costs: Licensing can range from R 5,000 to R 50,000 or more, depending on specific circumstances.
    • Timeline: The process can take 3 to 12 months or longer.
